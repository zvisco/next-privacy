No accounts · No tracking · No servers — your data never leaves your Mac

Everything you track, finally together

Events and to-dos in one place — quick to add, easy to read, and entirely yours.

Type it naturally

Just write what's on your mind. Lunch with Sam 1pm Friday #Work becomes a real event — dates, times, durations, lists and recurrence, all understood as you type.

Private by design

Next works directly with your Mac's built-in Calendar and Reminders. No account to create, no servers, no analytics — nothing is ever sent to us.

Every view you need

Switch between Week, Month, Agenda, and a dedicated Tasks board — each one drawn in your real calendar colors, in light or dark mode.

Tasks that keep up

Give to-dos a priority and due date, snooze them with a click, and set them to repeat. Group your list by date, list, or priority.

A calendar that moves at your pace

Drag an event to reschedule, pinch to zoom the day in or out, and let overlapping meetings split into clean side-by-side columns. The week always fills the screen — no dead space.

Add anything in seconds

The quick-add bar previews exactly what it will create as you type — the date, time, list, and a calendar to pick from — so you can capture a plan and move on.

Your to-dos, organized

A focused Tasks board groups everything by date, list, or priority — with overdue, today, tomorrow and upcoming always clear at a glance, and completed work one toggle away.

See the big picture

Zoom out to the Month for the shape of your weeks, or scroll the Agenda for a clean, chronological list of what's coming — events and tasks side by side.

Looks great, day or night

Light, dark, or follow your system automatically. Either way, your calendar and list colors come straight from macOS.

Your calendar stays yours

Next has no servers and no account system. It reads and writes your events and reminders locally through Apple's EventKit — the only thing it ever sends over the network is the location text you type, to Apple Maps, for address suggestions.
